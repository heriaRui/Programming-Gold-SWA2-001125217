Literature Lovers' Exchange

The Literature Lover's Club is an event for literature lovers on campus, where you can talk to other book lovers, share literature tips, discuss any literature-related topic and be sure to find like-minded friends!

The format of the activity is a discussion group of 5-8 people, you can bring your own books or use e-books, bring your reading notes, you can also prepare some small cards, the organisers have also prepared some fun games for you, I hope you can find your friends and happiness in the world of literature. (We accept the sharing of literature from any part of the world)

Clubbing Nights - A weekly party not to be missed!

Welcome to Durham University's Nightlife Centre, a venue designed for thrill-seeking students who enjoy spending time with friends. Every Friday night, our nightclub transforms into a sea of revelry, with a rotating programme of different themed parties, providing nightly entertainment like you've never experienced before. Nightclub Nights is a specially curated series of events designed to provide a platform for relaxation, excitement and socialising so that students can find ways to unwind and have fun outside of the pressures of studying. Whether you're a fan of electronic music, a fan of retro style or an enthusiastic dancer, there's a night for you. Themed nights cover a wide range of styles and music genres ensuring everyone can get stuck into this unrivalled experience. Join the club night events and make each weekend an unforgettable part of your time at the University. We're here for you.

How to attend Attending a club night is very simple. Tickets can be booked online or, exceptionally, can be purchased from our Student Union. We recommend purchasing tickets in advance to ensure that you can attend the themed night you are looking forward to. In addition, follow our website and social media platforms for the latest news and special offers. Typically: advance tickets £5, on the door £8 Tickets: visit the fixr events website or buy direct on the door of the club

About Us

The web designer is a current Durham University Computing student, only to provide our students with the convenience of attending the event, here you can not worry about the security, about privacy, it is not recommended to leave too much private information on the site.

Durham Events Society is a community that focuses on organising events, encouraging people to take part in them, and finding partners and friends for them to meet. We bring you weekly information about events and a space to rate them!

Basically, we only organise online, we are an online based event group, we have WhatsApp groups and other contacts, but sometimes we have society offline gatherings, it all depends!.

About Rules

Here are some rules that we might let you know before use this site

Respect and courtesy

Encourage community members to treat each other with respect, regardless of culture, region, colour, courtesy and basic respect in communication and participation in activities.

Authenticity of information

Any event promoter or participant must provide true and accurate information, both personal and event related.

Compliance with laws and regulations

All activities must comply with local laws and regulations and no illegal activities are allowed.

Keep the environment clean and tidy

If the event involves a physical location, participants should keep the venue clean and respect the public or private space used.
